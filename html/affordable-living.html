The living costs in Kyoto city vary based on personal choices, yet students can cut expenses by making wise decisions in transportation, food purchases, and choosing affordable accommodation options.

Food

The expenses for food for international students depend significantly on their individual preferences and lifestyle choices. However, they can effectively minimize these costs by making thoughtful decisions about where and how they purchase their food. Students can follow a few steps to minimize these costs:

  • Cooking your own meals.
  • Using school dining options, such as the cafeteria.
  • Shopping for groceries at more budget-friendly markets like Gyomu Super and Hanamasa.

Transportation

Kyoto's public transportation includes trains and buses, with the Kyoto City bus being the primary daily transportation option due to its extensive route network. As of March 2024, the fare for Kyoto City buses within the flat fare section is 230 yen. A commuter pass costs 8,280 yen per month or 44,710 yen for six months. If college and part-time job are nearby, students can significantly reduce transportation costs by using a bicycle.

Accommodation

Accommodation costs vary depending on individual preferences and interests. However, these costs can be minimized by choosing from the following options:

  • Residences provided by your language school or university.
  • Renting an apartment through a real estate agency.
  • International student residences or shared housing managed by governmental or NGO organizations.
